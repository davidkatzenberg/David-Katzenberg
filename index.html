Sustainable Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering work deals with maintaining and designing the foundations and general infrastructure of society. Whether it's the bridge you drive your car over on to the way to work or the water you drink, chances are a Civil Engineer had something to do with the physical design of that system or process. My current career aspirations are to take my Civil Engineering degree and put a sustainable and entrepreneurial twist on it. One day I hope to open a start up business focussed on solving environmental issues through the lense of Civil Engineering. For example, CO2 emissions are largely attributed to longstanding systems and processes such as transportation, energy production, and pollution, so from my perspective it makes sense to work towards improving these systems with the hope of improving our environmental situation. The main driving forces behind my career path choices have been my mom and my high school AP Environmental Science teacher. My mom used to work for Clean Water Action, a nonprofit which advocates for water quality and standards, which instilled in me an understanding of the environment's importance to society from a young age. My AP Enviro teacher helped further direct me towards envrionmental issues by passionately and clearly depicting current sustainability problems and their undervalued importance to society during class. I hope that one day I will use my degree to help people by solving environmental issues in the Civil Engineering field.

School

The engineering program at Pitt helped to show me how I could reach my goals of solving environmental issues through engineering. If I had to choose a few big learning takeaways from my college experience so far it would be: improved work ethic, more consistent confidence, analytical problem solving, and a clearer career plan. I hope to use these takeaways to further my engineering career and help improve the lives of others.
